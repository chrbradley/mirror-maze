Mirror Maze

Virtual Rooms & Light Paths

Explore how light travels from objects to your eye through multiple mirror reflections. When you look in a mirror, you see virtual images that appear to exist in "virtual rooms" behind the mirror surface.

The Physics: Light from the object bounces off mirrors following the law of reflection (angle of incidence equals angle of reflection) before reaching your eye. Your brain interprets this light as coming from a straight line, creating a virtual image. The virtual object appears to be located at a distance equal to the total length of the actual bouncing light path - demonstrating how our perception of distance is directly tied to the path light travels.

Both rays shown connect the same points but reveal different aspects of reflection: the dotted line shows where you perceive the object to be, while the solid line shows where the light actually traveled.

What you're seeing:

  • Dotted line: Your line of sight to the virtual image
  • Solid line: The actual path light takes, bouncing off room walls to reach you
  • Shapes: Triangle (object) and circle (your eye/receptor)

Interactive controls:

  • Click the HOME button to choose which room contains the object and receptor
  • Click the TARGET button to select which virtual room you're looking at
  • Drag the triangle and circle within the home room to see how reflections change
  • Notice how both rays always connect the same points, revealing the geometry of reflection
